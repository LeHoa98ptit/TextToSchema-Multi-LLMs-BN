The database is an inpatient information management system used to record inpatient information, medical processes, and manage related resources (such as wards, departments, doctors, etc.). The system assists hospitals in efficiently managing patient hospitalization status, doctors' treatment processes, ward resource usage, and other information to ensure smooth medical operations. The system primarily includes the following tables:

1. Patient  
Fields: Patient ID, Name, Gender, Age, Hospitalization Time, Ward, Phone, Identity Card Number, Medical Record Number, Emergency Contact Phone  
Business Requirement: Record basic patient information, including patient name, contact information, hospitalization time, and ward, etc. Through this table, hospitals can manage and query basic patient details to facilitate subsequent treatment, medical advice, and tracking of medical records.

2. Department  
Fields: Department ID, Department Name, Department Head ID, Contact Phone, Location  
Business Requirement: Record information of each hospital department, including department name, head, and department location, etc. Through this table, hospitals can manage departments and allocate resources such as doctors and nurses.

3. Doctor  
Fields: Doctor ID, Name, Gender, Contact Phone, Title, Department ID  
Business Requirement: Record doctor information, including name, title, contact information, and department affiliation. The doctor table is associated with the department table to help hospitals manage doctor resources and arrange patient consultations.

4. Ward  
Fields: Ward ID, Location, Head ID, Bed Count, Remaining Bed Count  
Business Requirement: Record basic ward information, including ward location, head (doctor), bed count, and remaining bed count. Through this table, hospitals can efficiently manage ward resources, allocate beds reasonably, and ensure patients can be accommodated promptly.

5. Nurse  
Fields: Nurse ID, Name, Gender, Phone, Department ID, Duties, Title  
Business Requirement: Record basic nurse information, including name, contact information, title, duties, and department affiliation. The nurse table is associated with the department table for managing nurse allocation, duties, and patient care relationships.
